Calculus is the study of change, and has two main ideas: differentiation, and integration.

Limits

Limits are used to figure out what value a function approaches as its input gets infinitely close to a specified value.

  1. Introduction to Limits: Notation and purpose.
  2. DNE: Figuring out when a limit doesn't exist.
  3. One-Sided Limits: Finding the limit from just one direction.
  4. Properties of Limits: Working with, or modifying, limits.
  5. Continuity: Using limits to check continuity at a point.
  6. Indeterminateness: Solving a limit with no clear answer.
 Derivatives I

  2. Limit Definition: How derivatives are actually defined.
  3. Properties of Derivatives: Working with derivatives.
  4. Power Rule Highlight: A quick method of differentiating polynomials.
  5. One-Sided Derivatives: Checking the derivative of just one side of a point.
  6. Differentiability: Figuring out when a function can't be differentiated at a given point.
  7. Derivative Rules: Easily find derivaties of different types of functions.
  8. Trigonometric Derivative Rules: Easily find derivatives of trigonometric functions.
